Hydrabad Biriyani

Description

A fragrant, flavorful, and spicy rice dish from Hyderabad, India, made with basmati rice, tender meat (chicken/mutton), and a blend of aromatic spices.

Ingredients:

For Marination:

  • 500g Chicken/Mutton(bone-in pieces)
  • 1 cup Yogurt (Dahi)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp Turmeric (Haldi)
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Powder (Dhania)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Biryani Masala(optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • Fresh Mint (Pudina) & Coriander Leaves (Dhania)(chopped, ¬Ω cup)

For Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati Rice(soaked for 30 mins)
  • 4 cups Water
  • 1 Bay Leaf (Tej Patta)
  • 4-5 Cloves (Laung)
  • 2-3 Green Cardamoms (Elaichi)
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
  • 1 Star Anise (optional)
  • Salt to taste

    • For Layering & Garnish:

    • 2 large Onions(thinly sliced & fried golden)
    • 1 large Tomato(sliced)
    • Saffron (Kesar)(soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
    • 2 tbsp Ghee/Clarified Butter
    • Fresh Mint & Coriander Leaves

    Steps:

    1. Marinate the Meat:
      • Mix chicken/mutton with all marination ingredients.
      • Cover & refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight for best flavor).
    2. Prepare Rice:
      • Boil water with whole spices & salt.
      • Add soaked rice, cook until 70% done (grains should be firm). Drain & set aside.
    3. Cook the Marinated Meat:
      • In a heavy-bottomed pan, cook marinated meat on low heat for 15-20 mins (until partially cooked).
    4. Layering (Dum Cooking):
      • In a heavy pot (handi/deep pan), layer:
        • Bottom:Half-cooked meat + some fried onions, mint, coriander.
        • Middle:Half-cooked rice + fried onions, saffron milk, ghee.
        • Repeat layers, ending with rice.
    5. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):
      • Cook on low heat for 20-25 mins (for chicken) or 35-40 mins (for mutton).
      • Let it rest for 10 mins before opening.
    6. Serve Hot:
      • Gently fluff the biryani, garnish with fried onions, mint, and lemon wedges.
      • Best enjoyed with Mirchi ka Salan or Raita!
